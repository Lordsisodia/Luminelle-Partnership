Animal Welfare and Nutrition Information

All ButcherBox beef is certified pasture-raised. This means animals are never confined to a feedlot and are able to roam freely and behave naturally on pasture. This roaming can function as a natural fertilizer and benefit soil and water health. We offer pasture-raised, 100% grass-fed beef from Australia and pasture-raised, grain-finished beef from the US to provide options for our members while also making sure the product we offer meets our rigorous sourcing standards. Our 100% grass-fed beef eat a natural diet of grass and forage on pasture for their entire lives; never confined to a feedlot. 100% grass-fed meat is lean, contains Omega-3's and has a robust flavor due to the animal's natural diet.Our grain-finished beef also eat grass and forage on pasture and are never confined to a feedlot. Grains are added to their diet at the end of their lives, resulting in increased marbling, tender texture and rich flavor.You can always be confident you’re getting what you picked— we clearly label whether a product is 100% grass-fed or grain-finished so you can see all the sourcing and standards for that product before you add it to your box.You can also learn more about our beef here.
Our cattle are certified pasture-raised which means they are never confined to a feedlot and have continuous access to pasture. This enables them to roam freely and behave naturally on pasture. We source two types of beef. Our 100% grass-fed cattle is grass-finished and eat a natural diet of grass their entire lives. Our grain-finished cattle are initially raised on pasture and eat grass and forages. Towards the end of their life, their diet expands to include hay, silage, and grain which is fed to the cattle while on pasture. Our pigs are raised crate-free and fed an all-vegetarian diet (with the exception of milk proteins). Our free-range, organic chickens eat USDA Certified Organic feed, which means it is grown with no synthetic pesticides, herbicides, animal byproducts, or GMOs. Free-range chickens have seasonal access to the outdoors where they can behave like chickens do, pecking, dust bathing, and exploring. Our pasture-raised, heirloom chickens are able to move freely and behave naturally on pasture.All our proteins are no antibiotics or added hormones ever. Shipping and Delivery

With our Custom Plan, you can choose exactly which cuts of meat you'll be getting in your order. You can edit your selections any time before the date your order bills.With our curated plans (Beef & Chicken, Beef & Pork, Mixed, or All Beef), you can choose whether you'd like beef, pork, and/or chicken, and we'll pick your specific cuts. You will find out what's in your order when you open it on delivery day—our members love the surprise!
Your box will be delivered within approximately 2-5 business days from the date your order bills. Here's a quick guide to our order and delivery timeline:
  • Your order will be packed with the optimal amount of dry ice so that your meat arrives cold.
  • Your order will ship 1-4 days from the date you place it (or 1-4 days after the date your order bills for recurring boxes).
  • As soon as your order ships, you'll receive an email with tracking information. You can also sign up for SMS notifications at butcherbox.com/sms.
  • Our carriers typically deliver between 8am-8pm, with a small number of deliveries occurring outside of those hours. Check your tracking information to follow your order!
You don't have to be home to sign for or accept your delivery, but all orders should be refrigerated or frozen as soon as possible.When delivered on time and retrieved within 6-8 hours of delivery, your ButcherBox products should arrive refrigerator cold—below 40°F as measured with a food thermometer. Your meats could arrive partly frozen (solid) or frozen and can be placed in the freezer for later use.
